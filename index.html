Why a Timeline?

Human evolution did not happen in a straight line from “ape” to “human.” Instead, it is a branching process that unfolds over tens of millions of years. This website visualizes key moments in primate and hominin evolution, showing how anatomical, behavioral, and ecological changes accumulate over deep time.

Scroll horizontally through the timeline below and click on each card to see detailed explanations that connect fossils, anatomy, and environment.

How this timeline connects to biological anthropology

This timeline is not just a list of names and dates. It highlights patterns that are central to biological anthropology:

  • Adaptation: changes in locomotion, teeth, and brain size reflect adaptation to new environments and diets.
  • Phylogeny: humans are nested within the primate order, and our closest relatives are African apes, not “less evolved humans.”
  • Mosaic evolution: traits like bipedalism, tool use, and big brains do not evolve all at once but in different combinations across species.
  • Deep time: the scale of millions of years challenges our everyday sense of time and shows how gradual change can produce dramatic differences.

As an Unessay, this website uses design, interaction, and visual structure to communicate the logic of human evolution rather than relying only on a traditional written essay.
